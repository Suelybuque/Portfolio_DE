"Exactly-once" is often sold as a checkbox on a streaming engine. In production it is a chain of invariants: durable logs, aligned checkpoints, idempotent sinks, and operational discipline when those invariants break.

## The chain

1. **Producers** write with idempotent configs and transactional IDs where fan-in requires it.
2. **Flink** checkpoints barrier-aligned state with `EXACTLY_ONCE` mode and sufficient timeout headroom.
3. **Sinks** are idempotent — upsert keys in Snowflake, SET operations in Redis, or transactional Kafka sinks.

Break any link and you are back to at-least-once with prettier dashboards.

## Failure modes we still watch

- Checkpoint timeouts during GC storms that silently fall back to at-least-once behavior
- Schema registry compatibility gaps that crash jobs mid-epoch
- Downstream unique-key violations that look like "duplicates" but are actually late retries

Exactly-once is achievable. It is also never "set and forget."
