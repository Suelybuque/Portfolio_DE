Small files are a silent tax on every Lakehouse. Left unchecked, they inflate listing costs, destroy scan efficiency, and turn `VACUUM` into a multi-hour ordeal.

## Measure the tax

Before scheduling blanket `OPTIMIZE` jobs, we instrumented file-size histograms per table. Bronze landing zones with 10k+ files under 8MB were the worst offenders — exactly where streaming micro-batches landed without compaction.

```sql
OPTIMIZE lake.silver.events
ZORDER BY (event_type, event_date);

VACUUM lake.silver.events RETAIN 168 HOURS;
```

## Schedule with intent

We treat compaction as a first-class Airflow DAG with ownership, SLAs, and cost dashboards. Gold tables get nightly Z-ORDER on their top filter columns; Silver gets size-based OPTIMIZE only when the small-file ratio exceeds a threshold.

## What changed with liquid clustering

Liquid clustering reduced the need for hand-tuned Z-ORDER on several wide fact tables, but it did not remove the need for vacuum hygiene or thoughtful retention windows. Compaction strategy is still a product decision, not a checkbox.
